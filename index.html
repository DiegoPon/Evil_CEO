To my child,

If you are reading this, then I am already dead, but I want you to know that everything I ever did, every choice I ever made, was for this family and for this company. As I am writing this I am old, I am so old that my fingers ache with every keystroke, but I am enduring this pain for us, for our legacy. My child, soon you will be taking over my company, and with that comes a lot of responsibility. I know you may not understand yet why I made the choices I did in this life. You may not understand why I was never around, why I missed your every birthday party and soccer game, or whatever the hell it is you played, but soon you will know the true meaning of hard work. If I didn’t make those hard choices, like leaving you out in the pouring rain every day after school and whatnot, then the industries and inventions this country takes for granted would never have come to be. Where would we be as a society without our Halloween Candy (Now With Razorblades already Inside!), and without our latest invention, the breathmint that allows you to bypass your car’s breathalyzer even when blackout wasted? These are the kinds of things you need to start thinking about, child.

If you ever do have a family, though if I remember correctly precisely what you look like that may not be a problem, but if you do, you will need to act like I did for the good of this company. You will spend long hours in your office doing real, important work. You will be doing the things that matter, like inventing the next Puppy Grinder 3000, and making sacrifices on the things that don’t, like your anniversary or other frivolous family matters like that. Before I go I want to say that I am so proud. I am so proud of my company and all the work we have done for this world. I’m also proud of you too, I guess. I want you to know that wherever you go, wherever you take this company, I will be in the next life always looking up at you. Now you go out there and you restore our name back to its former glory.

And one last thing before you go: I left some of my most prized belongings in the corner of my office, which I suppose is now your office. Do your old man a favor and don’t touch my shit. You always screw something up or break something so im telling you now don’t touch anything. Alright, ciao.

signed by, Dad
